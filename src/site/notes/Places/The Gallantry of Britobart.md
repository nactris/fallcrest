#living_dungeon

A fairy tale dungeon. Wizards never managed to collapse it. The dungeon follows an epic story of Britobart. While it was mostly searched through people sometimes still find magical items in it. 

People cannot die there, they just spit ton of blood and dramatically feint, torn clothes and so on. 
In this dungeon Britobart is a villain and a woman, instead of heroic man. Still has a shiny armour.

Popular destination for young wizards to practice.

Easiest way to get there is absentmindedly read a facsimile of the story, in particular point the door will appear, on the door the bird will sit and ask a question. As long as the intent to answer is present any answer will do. However if you refuse the idea of question the door will not allow you through. Don't do that.

 The crow at the doors asked for true names.


> [!NOTE] Mira's Riddle
>  *I am Mira Rosalis, the Unbound. Name given to me by ones I love the most.*
> 
> You are an armoire. You have held many things over the years but recently you were emptied by a man you'd never seen before. You have been emptied and filled by many caretakers over your years of service but this was the first one that apologized for leaving you so barren. As proof of his contrition he grants you control over your doors and tells you you get to decide from now on who gets to open them. Choice is a new and terrifying concept to you but you are, ironically, not given the opportunity to choose to accept this gift or not: it is bestowed. Later that evening an unknown guest approaches your doors. They look desperate, like they should not be here, and you feel as though to allow them entry would fray the fabric of reality and to not would be to ensure their agonizing death at your wooden feet. Do you let them in?
> 
> *Yes, I do.*



> [!NOTE] Ginger's Riddle
>*Some call me Ginger or Gingersnap, Mrs. Markelhay, I was also called Lily*
>
> Your beloved partner has died, and every time you visit their grave to place flowers, you cannot find the marker in the thick mist that seems to form every time you arrive in the graveyard. Frustrated, you endeavor to stay in the night in the graveyard, hidden, determined to stay until the mist fades. In your vigil slumber overtakes you. You awaken to the sound of your partner's spectre haggling payment for future mists with a nereid (nee·ree·uhd), a sea nymph. They seem to have been caught off guard by the frequency of your visits. How do you react to this apparent betrayal?
> 
> *I would feel heartbroken, if I loved this person, I'd trust they had their reasons and I will give them their space.*



> [!NOTE] Zim's Riddle
> *Zimrial ....... the Alluring*
> 
> You are a lady in waiting, and have decided you are too young to know what suitor you should choose. You decide to don magical masks that will exaggerate aspects of your own personality, hoping you will find a truer love with a more mature instinct for boldness. To your distress, you fall in love with 2 suitors in equal measure, each desiring a different mask. You are left with a dilemma, choose one mask and suitor and be sure to break the other's heart, or be yourself and see neither of them again, but spare them the heartache of love unrequited. What do you choose?

> [!NOTE] Yonder's Riddle
> *Yonder. A name chosen for myself* 
>
> You are a street cat, nimble and wise. A local baker leaves out a saucer of milk every morning and you lap it up with glee. It is your favorite part of each day. One night, a wilder cat from alleys unknown arrives in the city, they have gathered a crowd to hear them speak and you grant them your adorable ears for a spell. They preach, informing the gathered litter that cats are the realm's true rulers but the two-legs have tricked us. They pacify our minds with scritches, wet meats, sun-drenched balconies, and most potent of all, saucers of creamy milk. His words touch you, but so did the baker's kindness. Do you continue to drink the milk?
> 
> *It is unhealthy for cats to drink milk past kittenhood*

> [!NOTE] Tobolar's Riddle
>*Tobolar Quickfoot, Wizard Apprentice, Last student of Nimozaran the Green. *
>
> You are a pie sitting on the windowsill of a cozy domicile in wanderhome. You are mincemeat and spice, your steam blows into the rolling hills, meadows, and cottages and this brings you satisfaction. You know that you are a waiting gift for the wife of a newly-wed halfling couple. This is the first time she has left home since their union was sealed by ribbon and cord and you are the recipe her mother tried to impart, though it was not written down so you are slightly off. A local baker catches sniff of you, and approaches the windowsill. "MMmm, this will not do little pie, there is not enough cinnamon and your top lacks the appropriate sugary glaze. With your permission, I will fix you right up and none will be the wiser." Do you accept his help? Tobolar looks to the party to answer.
> 
> *I will not accept, if the recipe was wrong then its a new recipe*


The action is stopped outside of small window around outsiders. 
The scene is bright and cheerful. People are shocked at Britobart's betrayal. 

We are led by a knight deeper into the castle. The people seem to know us by different names. 
1. Ironwood, Druid of Legend (Yonder)
2. Lyra the Quill (Zim)
3. Barnabas the Mad (Toblerone)
4. Vallorie the Fox (Mira) 
5. Morhana the Scholar of Death (Ginger)

We step into a corridor, [[PCs/Mira Rosalis\|Mira Rosalis]] finds a crack in the wall and pulls it apart, entering the library.
It stretches infinitely fractally warping and folding onto itself. There were the books there, that changed gravity, and [[PCs/Gingersnap Myra\|Ginger]] found a special one.
Special book was able to play sound from what was written into it. The sound was what the writer expected. 

After we leave we enter infinite hallway filled with paintings. It stretches forward but loops. 
The paintings depict a cheerful villagers and generous king. 
They are protected with magic, they blast Mira away when she tries to turn them upside down.
The paintings had two layers, and after peeling upper one [[PCs/Yonder\|Yonder]] revealed drastic change. 
People were not cheerful, they were terrified, and king was greedy and cruel.

After that the hallway led to the throne room where the king and Britobart were waiting.
King is tied and Britobart does not accept discussion. Let the blades speak.

We wrote the partial ending:

The quill stills, we get paralyzed we cannot continue writing.

rest should be:
